Summary

ML/AI engineer with a first-principles approach to machine learning systems. I prefer to construct models from the ground up — implementing forward passes, deriving gradients by hand, and writing training loops without abstraction — over composing high-level API calls. My conviction is that genuine understanding of a system requires having built it, and that the mathematical substrate of a model is not separable from its engineering reality.

My primary technical focus is deep learning, with active work in natural language processing, large language models, and computer vision. Alongside this, I hold a developing interest in quantum computing — studied currently at the level of quantum circuits and gate-model computation via Qiskit. I am building toward a research-grade understanding of the mathematics that governs modern neural architectures: probability theory, real analysis, and the linear-algebraic foundations of optimization.

I am a self-directed learner enrolled in Computer Engineering at two institutions simultaneously. My study methodology combines formal coursework, independent reading, and implementation-as-proof — a project does not count as understood until it is built.
